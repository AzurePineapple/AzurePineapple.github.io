What is the dataset?

This dataset contains Pokémon encounter data from a script (lovingly referred to as a `Shiny Hunting Bot' or simply `bot') operated by the youtube channel 40 Cakes, playing through Pokémon Emerald, and has been for 24 hours a day since the 1st January 2023. It is expected to run for years to come. The bot's objective is to complete a combination of challenges:

Professor Oak's Challenge:
This challenge requires you to catch every Pokémon available to you before you can fight the next Gym leader (the game mechanics that gate progression). This includes evolving Pokémon as far as possible.
Living Dex:
The objective of the Living Dex challenge is to have one of each Pokémon in your boxes, including each stage of evolution. A three stage evolution Pokémon will require you to capture 3 each of the first stage. For example, you need to capture 3 Trapinch, one to stay as it is, one to evolve to Vibrava, and another to evolve to Flygon.
Shiny Dex:
The Shiny Dex challenge mandates that each captured Pokémon must be a Shiny. A Shiny Pokémon has different colouring than the normal Pokémon (see image on right — The top shows the usual colouring of Ampharos, the bottom shows the Shiny sprite). Each Pokémon has a 1 in 8192 chance of being Shiny.

40 Cakes has their own explanation of the challenge, available here.

Where is this data from?

The bot outputs encounter data to publicly accessible .csv files every five minutes; each file in the dataset contains encounter data for each Phase (see glossary). Each encounter contains a wealth of data, 65 different values ranging from the date of the encounter to the unique statistics of that specific Pokémon. It is from these 2,036,406 wild Pokémon encounters that our visualisations were created.

Dataset difficulties

Old Formatting:
The earliest iterations of the bot stored encounter data in a far less organised manner, and much of this data failed to include important values such as the date of the encounter, and other values that were critical in creating the desired visualisations. Phases 1-125 were stored in this manner, and as such were unusable for this project.
Constant updates:
Due to the constant updates to this live dataset, the largest possible subset of the available data was used for the visualisations at the time of this project's beginning. 375 Phases had been completed on 14/05/2024 (when the data was taken). Excluding the data from before Phase 126 yields a total of 250 Phases worth of data, totalling 2,036,406 wild Pokémon encounters.
Corrupt Data:
During the development of the project it was noticed that some values were appearing on visualisations that should not have been possible, such as the bot encountering a Charizard at Route 101 and a Poochyena at Sky Pillar — a Pokémon and a location respectively that are both impossible to have encountered, given the bot's current level of progress through the challenge. Initial steps were taken to ignore these corrupt files, but it was eventually observed that these corrupt encounter entries coincided with the encounter having Pokérus — either infected or cured. Once this observation was made, it was decided to omit all data entries with a positive Pokérus status, as a very small fraction (1 in 21,845) of the encounters.
Dirty Data:
In addition to some encounter's having corrupt, unusable data, a number of Phase's data had formatting issues in some rows; usually the bot had written an extra value for one of the 65 different attributes, resulting in a row that was longer than expected. These datasets had to be painstakingly examined and repaired by hand in a meticulous manual process.

The Seedot Problem

Seedot is a Pokémon that can appear on Route 102 in Pokémon Emerald. The rules of the challenge being attempted by the bot stipulate that it must obtain every Pokémon it is able to before each Gym, in its Shiny form. Unfortunately for the bot, Seedot only has a 1% chance of being encountered on Route 102, and has only a 1 in 8192 chance of being Shiny. These combined odds mean that each encounter on Route 102 has a 1 in 819,200 of being a Shiny Seedot. The challenge requires that two be caught before the first Gym, as it is possible to evolve Seedot into Nuzleaf before completing this Gym. Fortunately, it is not possible to evolve Nuzleaf before the first Gym, so only two are required at this stage.

The first Seedot was found on the 7th June 2023, after 3 Months and 20 Days of non-stop grinding. Unfortunately, this occurs outside of our usable data range, though we have isolated the encounter in the old data, and 40 Cakes has a video of the encounter.

The hunt for the second Seedot began on the 27th August 2023, and as of yet has not been completed.

Glossary of terms

Encounter:
A battle with a wild Pokémon. Each encounter is random, the type of Pokémon encountered varies depending on which in-game location the player is in. Encounters are found in long grass or in caves.
IV's
Individual Values, 6 values ranging from 0 to 31 that affect the 6 stats of a Pokémon. More information found here.
Phase:
A term used in the Pokémon Shiny hunting community to refer to the number of Pokémon encountered since the last encounter with a shiny. Sometimes interpreted as an acronym, P.H.A.S.E: Player Has A Shiny Encounter. Ultimately, these two definitions have the same end meaning. Each Phase's final encounter is with a Shiny.
Phase Shiny Probability:
Using a binomial distribution model, the probability = B(n,p) that after n encounters, each with an independent chance p of being a shiny, a shiny will be encountered.
Pokérus:
A beneficial `virus' that wild Pokémon can have (1 in 21,845) and inflict upon the player's Pokémon. It doubles the effort values gained from battling wild Pokémon. More information can be found here.
Shiny Hunting Bot | Bot | Script:
The program developed by 40 Cakes that is the focus of the stream. It is attempting to complete the Professor Oak's Shiny Living Dex challenge.
Shiny Pokémon
A rare instance of a Pokémon, with a different color scheme than normal. Has a 1 in 8192 chance of occurring. More details found here.
Streak
The number of consecutive encounters with the same Pokémon
